PROBLEM STATEMENT

Heart disease is the leading cause of death worldwide, accounting for one third of deaths in 2019. Heart disease cases nearly doubled over the period, from 271 million in 1990 to 523 million in 2019 and the number of fatalities from heart disease increased by 50 percent, from 12 million to 18 million. The shocking fact is 3 out of 4 such deaths took place in developing countries countries.

However, early detection of cardiovascular diseases, adequate medication, and specialist consultation can lower the chance of fatality by 90%. Good data-driven approaches for predicting cardiac diseases have the potential to enhance the entire research and preventive process. In this project, the effort is given to determine whether the patient has 10-year risk of future coronary heart disease (CHD) or not. We have created a tool that can estimate the risk of CHD depending on a person's medical history using machine learning approaches.

Dataset Info

The dataset is from an cardiovascular study on residents of the town of Framingham, Massachusetts; Available publicly in Kaggle.
Target variable is binary; Total Negative labeled count is 2483 and total Positive labeled count is 444.
The total percentage of missing data is 15.04%

Demographic Attributes info

  • sex: male or female("M" or "F")
  • age: Age of the patient (Continous)
  • education: No further information provided

Behavioral Attributes Info

  • is_smoking: whether or not the patient is a current smoker ("YES" or "NO")
  • Cigs Per Day: the number of cigarettes that the person smoked on average in one day (Continous)

Medical Histories Info

  • BP Meds: whether or not the patient was on blood pressure medication (Nominal)
  • Prevalent Stroke: whether or not the patient had previously had a stroke (Nominal)
  • Prevalent Hyp: whether or not the patient was hypertensive (Nominal)
  • Diabetes: whether or not the patient had diabetes (Nominal)
  • Tot Chol: total cholesterol level (Continuous)
  • Sys BP: systolic blood pressure (Continuous)
  • Dia BP: diastolic blood pressure (Continuous)
  • BMI: Body Mass Index (Continuous)
  • Heart Rate: heart rate (Continuous)
  • Glucose: glucose level (Continuous)

Features Selection

First extracted top 8 features using 2 methods described below, then selected the Common Features from the two methods.

  • BorutaPy
  • SelectKBest

Boruta is an all-relevant wrapper feature selection method. It is an all relevant feature selection method, while most other are minimal optimal; this means it tries to find all features carrying information usable for prediction, rather than finding a possibly compact subset of features on which some classifier has a minimal error. BorutaPy package runs Random Forest on both original and random attributes.

Link to BorutaPy Papers

Selected Features using BorutaPy

age cigsPerDay totChol sysBP diaBP BMI heartRate glucose

SelectKBest will select the K most explicative features out of the original set. k is best k number of features that the algorithm returns to us. This method also takes parameter score_func which defines the statistical method to use. We've used chi2 test as our scoring function. It is available on sklearn library.

Fabrication of ML Pipelines

We focused on building pipelines in order to increase reproducibility and prevent data leakage. We've trained models on 2 different pipelines as follows

Base Pipeline

No resampling was attempted. Training was carried out on 6 different models, with hyperparameters fine-tuned via 10-fold cross-validation. Steps are defined as below,

Data Normalization

  1. StandardScaler is used to scale attributes to unit variance

Cross Validation

  1. 10 folds ShuffleSplit
  2. Hyperparameters tuned with HalvingGridSearchCV

Resampled pipeline

Dataset is oversampled with SMOTE first with sample ratio of 0.8. Then Random Undersampling is applied to the output of the SMOTE transform before returning the final outcome. Then same defined procedures of first pipeline is repeated.

Data Resampling

  1. SMOTE is used to oversample the minority target labels.
  2. RandomUnderSampler is used to allow transforms to be stacked or applied in sequence on dataset

Data Normalization

  1. StandardScaler is used to scale attributes to unit variance

Cross Validation

  1. 10 folds ShuffleSplit
  2. Hyperparameters tuned with HalvingGridSearchCV

  With the goal of assisting the Healthcare Industry, this project reliably fulfils the objective of diagnosing persons at high risk of CHD, or coronary heart disease. CHD itself is accounts for 31% deaths worldwide. One of the shocking fact is 3 out of 4 such deaths took place in third world countries. These countries like India still haven’t fully adapted the use of machine learning but adapting at the highest rate within all over the world. Thus, it leaves us a huge scope to leverage the power of predictive modelling.
  Our project has business scope in both healthcare industry and financial institutions. In healthcare industry our project can deliver automated health checkup which may explicitly be used as a paid service or implicitly asses one patient’s condition before checkup. We believe this will precisely help the industry to grow fast paced and revolutionize the treatment. As well as the financial institutions, who provides medical insurance and loans can use our tool to conduct risk assessment before to lower the risk of defaulting.

With a proper implementation in industry, we believe our tool has the potential to,

  1. Usage as a SaaS type of tool, can be implemented as a single or combining with an automated health checkup package.
  2. Periodical usage to quickly asses one patient’s condition by medical institution, which can alert for any noticeable changes of one’s medical risk factors, allowing doctors to treat more precisely.
  3. This tool is capable of assessing risk of CHD for the next 10 years. Financial institutions can assess the risk based on one’s medical condition before providing loan or insurances using our tool.
  4. Data is the future. By implementing our tool in public domains, we can collect random data from people of different racial and demographic location. It can be further analyzed leaving an open score for future.

  1. Imbalanced Dataset: The dataset we’ve worked on is imbalanced in nature. We’ve only 15% of our data belongs from minority classes. Most common unwanted situation we could face from such a dataset is that our could score 85% accuracy by always predicting majority classes. It is certainly not the desired way we want our model to work.
    In order to solve the problem, we’ve approached for the generation of synthetic minority class data points using popular synthetic minority oversampling technique or SMOTE where the minority class is up-sampled by choosing a datapoint, randomly weighting its nearest neighbor, and adding that distortional neighbor to the original sample.
    Using the SMOTE approach, we got a significant 55% increase in recall using Random Forest model.
  2. Deployment to Heroku: Because it is my first Python project that I've deployed on Heroku, I've encountered a few issues during deployment. I’ve efficiently debug my app followed by reading documentations and articles and solved these all.
    Some issues I’d like to mention is selection of ports used by Heroku. I was constantly getting error log of a particular port is being occupied by some other process. I solved that by adding a dynamic $PORT variable in Procfile.
    One other issue that had given me headache is getting permission denied error in Heroku while my app was running perfectly in local. After a through debug of few hours, I’ve identified the problem by changing in my flask run script. I’ve learned how Heroku is using gunicorn server to execute a python script and the importance of __name__ variable in Python.

  For the future scope, the first thing comes in my mind is to improve our model or try out other approaches which could potentially increase the recall metrics. We can try some other normalization method like MinMaxScaler etc. Also, we do believe other ensemble learning methods like XGBoost, CatBoost or AdaBoost can increase performance effectively but with the cost of more computation.
  Currently, most of the positive cases we’ve worked with are artificially synthesized data points. So, we should focus on adding more real-life data from positive cases of CHD. This will generalize our model to use in real-life scenario more proficiently
